Rural Outreach Workshops

A student-led initiative bringing design, web development, and digital literacy training to underrepresented rural youth in India.

Project Hero Image

Project Overview

During my academic journey, I launched a series of rural outreach workshops focused on bridging the digital divide. These offline sessions empowered students and aspiring creators in remote areas by introducing them to design thinking, UI/UX, web development, and basic tech literacy.

The workshops were conducted under the Avoneme umbrella or independently, with a goal to democratize access to digital tools and plant seeds of innovation in regions with untapped potential.

Workshop Highlights

Design & UI Basics

Introduced youth to interface design using paper prototyping and basic Figma workflows adapted to offline learning.

Intro to Web Development

Covered core HTML/CSS concepts, using live demos and simplified tools even on low-spec devices.

Local Language Delivery

Sessions were customized in Hindi or the regional language for better clarity, inclusion, and engagement.

Career Awareness

Shared free online resources, scholarships, and career paths in tech and design for rural students to explore.

Impact & Goals

So far, I’ve conducted 3+ sessions across villages in Uttar Pradesh, directly impacting over 80+ students. These workshops have sparked curiosity, boosted confidence, and opened doors to digital self-learning in regions with limited exposure.

My goal is to scale this initiative during my gap year — conducting 10+ such workshops, building a scalable toolkit for other volunteers, and creating partnerships with NGOs for long-term reach.
