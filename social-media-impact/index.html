The Impact of Social Media on Rural Youth

A research paper exploring how social media influences the mindset, education, habits, and aspirations of children in rural India.

Research Paper Cover

Project Overview

This research project was initiated during my school outreach programs in rural areas of Uttar Pradesh. I noticed a growing presence of smartphones and social media, even among young kids in villages.

The paper examines the psychological, educational, and social effects of platforms like YouTube, WhatsApp, and Instagram on rural students aged 10–16. The goal was to assess both the benefits and hidden risks of early digital exposure.

Methodology

Survey-Based

Interviewed 70+ students across 3 villages using structured and open-ended questions.

Parental Perspectives

Included responses from 20+ parents to compare adult perceptions of social media's role.

Offline + Observational

Used direct observations and informal chats to understand context and behavior patterns.

Key Findings

Digital Curiosity

87% of kids interviewed had used YouTube, mostly for entertainment — but many also watched educational content like DIY science.

Screen Time

Average screen time ranged from 1.5 to 4 hours per day, often without parental supervision or structure.

Aspirational Shift

Some children wanted to become vloggers or gamers — showing how online role models are reshaping ambitions.

Awareness Gap

Majority lacked awareness of digital addiction, misinformation, or online safety practices.

Solutions & Impact

The research concludes with practical recommendations: introducing basic digital literacy modules in rural schools, organizing community talks on healthy screen habits, and encouraging platforms like SolveMyIndia to spread awareness.

This paper is being compiled into a formal journal submission and will also be available as a downloadable PDF for educators and civic tech platforms.
